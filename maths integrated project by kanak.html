Who was Srinivasa Ramanujan?

Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887–1920) was one of the greatest mathematicians in history. Born in Tamil Nadu, India, he showed extraordinary talent in mathematics from a young age. Despite facing many challenges, his passion for numbers led him to discover groundbreaking formulas and theories.

His Amazing Contributions

  • Ramanujan Prime: Special numbers that are important in number theory.
  • Partition Theory: A way to divide numbers into smaller parts with fascinating patterns.
  • The Number 1729: Known as the "Hardy-Ramanujan number," it is the smallest number expressible as the sum of two cubes in two different ways.
  • Infinite Series: He discovered unique series for π and other constants, which are still used in modern math.

Why is He Remembered?

Ramanujan's work continues to influence mathematics today. His collaboration with British mathematician G. H. Hardy brought his ideas to the global stage. He is celebrated for his deep intuition and ability to solve complex problems without formal training.

Fun Fact!

The movie The Man Who Knew Infinity tells the story of Ramanujan’s journey from India to Cambridge University and his incredible contributions to mathematics.
